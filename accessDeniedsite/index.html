About IETE

The Institution of Electronics and Telecommunication Engineers (IETE) is India's leading recognized professional society devoted to the advancement of Science and Technology of Electronics, Telecommunication and IT. The Institution provides leadership in Scientific and Technical areas of direct importance to the national development and economy. Government of India has recognized IETE as a Scientific and Industrial Research Organization (SIRO) and also notified as an educational Institution of national eminence. The objectives of IETE focus on advancing electro-technology. The IETE conducts and partners technical meetings, conferences, symposia, and exhibitions all over India, publishes technical journals and provides continuing education as well as career advancement opportunities to its members.

About Access Denied

AccessDenied is a 36-hour hackathon organized by IETE-ISF for innovators across the country. It is an great opportunity to put your technical skills to use. Apply your textbook knowledge to real life ideas and stand a chance to win exclusive goodies, schwags, certificates and cash prizes upto Rs. 20,000. Mark your calendars for 23th and 24th March 2019. Access Denied looks forward to promote; ”Surpassing the Vision”, which not only embraces one's desire to become better, but also enables one to be able to explain the myriad of emotions a person experiences on the path to success and spread the word.

Defence

1. DEF1-Design a surveillance system to support the activities for border security forces-Aim of any surveillance system is to provide observation into our Area of Responsibility and also to provide early warning. Elevated platform enhance the ranges as well as cover dead grounds, however the problem still persist. Expected Deliverables: To overcome shortcoming in present Battle Field Surveillance Radar. Extend surveillance ranges. Increase efficiency of existing systems.

2. DEF2-Design a Prototype of Autonomous bot which can assist the troops during combat for equipment services- Sometimes soldiers step on mines and get serious injuries so the bot can detect the presence of land mines. During covert operations by commandos the bot can be used to view the inside activity of the conflict area without getting noticed by the enemy troops. Expected Deliverables: The solution should lay emphasis on how the bot can be used in the enhancement of defence technologies. It should guarantee the safety of the soldiers and provide assistance in battle and operations as well.

3. DEF3-Identification of friend or foe (iff) system for armoured fighting vehicles IFF system is a tool of Combat Identification, “the process of attaining an accurate characterization of detected objects in the operational environment sufficient to support an engagement decision”. It also contributes to the overall tactical decision making. Expected Deliverables: To positively identify the opposing armoured vehicle prior to engagement. On battlefield, Armoured fighting vehicles would be required to engage enemy armoured fighting vehicles in time critical situations necessitating shortening of decision loop for engagement without positive identification of friend or foe which at times lead to fratricide by friendly fires.

Who is this hack for?

We encourage and welcome undergraduate students of all backgrounds to join us.

What if I don’t know how to code?

Interest in learning and working with technology is much more important than your current experience level. No experience is needed — you will be able to work with various mentors, interact with companies, and learn alongside fellow participants.

What if I don't have a team?

You can register individually and approach other teams if you would like to collaborate.

Where is this?

The Hack will be at Vellore Institute of Technology, Vellore near Katpadi Railway station or a 2 and a hour hour drive from Chennai.

What should I bring?

A valid student-id, a laptop and any hardware you'd require.

How much does this cost?

Even though the price isn't set yet, we're trying to make it accessible to everyone.
